# OneSource Cloud – Style Guide

This guide defines writing conventions, formatting standards, and editorial guidelines for all OneSource Cloud content.

---

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Capitalization

**Headlines & Subheadings**
- Use: Sentence case  
- Example:  
  - Private AI infrastructure for healthcare  

---

### Product Names

- OneSource Cloud → Always capitalized  
- OnePlus → Capitalized  
- Private AI Infrastructure → capitalized when used as category  

---

### Industry Terms

- AI infrastructure  
- GPU cluster  
- private AI  
- public cloud  
- compliance  
- data sovereignty  

---

## Word Choice & Usage

### Preferred Terms

**Say This → Not That**

- private AI infrastructure → AI platform  
- dedicated GPUs → scalable compute  
- predictable cost → cost optimization  
- fully managed → supported  
- data control → data flexibility  

---

### Words to Avoid

- cutting-edge  
- revolutionary  
- innovative solution  

---

## Formatting Standards

### Bold

Use for:
- Key concepts  
- Keywords  

---

### Lists

- Parallel structure  
- Max 5 items  
- Short and scannable  

---

### Links

- Use descriptive anchor text  
- Avoid “click here”  

---

## Content Structure

### Introduction

1. Hook  
2. Problem  
3. Shift  
4. Direction  

---

### Section Rules

- 2–4 sentences per paragraph  
- One idea per paragraph  
- 150–300 words per section  

---

### Conclusion

Must include:
- Summary  
- Decision guidance  
- CTA  

---

## SEO Rules

### Keyword Placement

- Title  
- First 100 words  
- Subheadings  
- Conclusion  

---

### Internal Links

Each article must include:

1. /private-ai-infrastructure  
2. One feature page  
3. One industry page  

---

## Voice & Tone

### Core Voice

- Clear  
- Decision-driven  
- Enterprise-focused  
- No hype  

---

### Tone by Content

**Strategy**
- Direct  
- Structured  

**Technical**
- Precise  
- Simplified  

**Industry**
- Risk-focused  
- Trust-driven  

---

## Enterprise Writing Rules

- Write for CTO / CIO  
- Focus on risk, cost, control  
- Avoid beginner-level explanations  

---

## Conversion Alignment

Content flow:

Blog → Feature → Private AI → Contact  

---

## Editing Checklist

- Primary keyword included  
- Internal links added  
- CTA present  
- Enterprise tone  
- No generic SaaS language  

---

## Principle

Content drives enterprise decisions toward private AI infrastructure.
