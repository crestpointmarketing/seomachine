## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Common Sections Across Top-Ranking Articles
- General definitions of digital sovereignty and data residency
- High-level overview of EU AI Act and GDPR
- Surface-level coverage of hyperscaler "sovereign cloud" offerings
- National AI strategy overviews (France, Germany, UAE, India)
- Geopolitical framing of AI competition between US, China, EU

### Content Gaps (Opportunities)
- **Practical architecture guidance**: Most articles discuss sovereignty as policy, not infrastructure. Few cover what sovereign AI architecture actually looks like (data plane isolation, jurisdiction-locked storage, in-country GPU clusters).
- **Sovereignty vs. residency vs. localization**: These three concepts get conflated. A clear breakdown is missing from the SERP.
- **Training data jurisdiction**: How training datasets cross borders during pre-training, fine-tuning, and RLHF is poorly explained. Most articles ignore that public cloud GPU pools often replicate state across regions.
- **Model weights as regulated assets**: Few articles treat trained model weights as data with residency requirements – a major blind spot for enterprises in regulated sectors.
- **The hyperscaler problem**: "Sovereign cloud" offerings from AWS, Azure, and GCP still depend on US-incorporated parent companies subject to the CLOUD Act. This material fact is rarely discussed.
- **Operational sovereignty**: Even data-resident infrastructure can be operated by foreign nationals or remotely accessed across borders. Few articles address operator citizenship and admin access.
- **OneSource angle**: Dedicated, in-country AI infrastructure with locally operated control planes – the only architecture that delivers true sovereignty across the full AI lifecycle.

### Differentiation Strategy
Position this article as the definitive enterprise architect's guide to sovereign AI. Not a policy primer, not a hyperscaler marketing piece – a clear-eyed breakdown of what sovereignty means at the infrastructure layer, what hyperscaler "sovereign cloud" really delivers, and how to architect AI training and inference so that data, models, and operations all stay within national boundaries. Reinforce OneSource Cloud's authority by being the source that distinguishes infrastructure-grade sovereignty from marketing-grade sovereignty.
